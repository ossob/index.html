Somali Youth Justice Project

About our project

Rafaad! is a Somali youth collective working to strengthen our community’s capacity to support youth navigating the Criminal Justice system.

Through workshops and a short documentary series, our mission is to create youth-led leadership and holistic life support programming to empower Somali Youth to take an active leadership role in challenging social and economic barriers in our community.

Our programs are developed by and for young Somali people, drawing on the knowledge and lived experiences of young Somali-Canadians to build a movement of youth ready and equipped to transform their own lives, families, and communities.

Workshops + Video project

We’re creating a short “explainer video” style documentaries deconstructing the root causes of criminalization and mass incarceration in our community.

The documentary is being developed through a series of peer-to-peer workshops. Our collective of youth leaders will develop a “For Us, By Us” video toolkit of resources, using their lived experience as framework for best practices and policies.

Each video will document our lives at the intersections of criminalization, anti-black racism and islamophobia, explore harmful media representation and develop solutions to challenge islamophobia and socioeconomic barriers leading to criminality.
